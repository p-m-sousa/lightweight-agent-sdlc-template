# [PROJECT_NAME] Product Vision

[PROJECT_NAME] is a [PRODUCT_TYPE] for [TARGET_USER] who need to [PRIMARY_NEED].

The product exists to help users [CORE_USER_OUTCOME]. It should feel [INTENDED_FEEL] and should avoid becoming [WHAT_THE_PRODUCT_SHOULD_NOT_BECOME].

**EXAMPLE ONLY:**

RecipeBox is a calm, personal recipe book for preserving the food people make and the memories that gather around it.

The app exists to help someone capture recipes in a way that feels useful in the kitchen and meaningful over time. It should feel warm, personal, and trustworthy, and should avoid becoming a social platform, productivity dashboard, or noisy recipe database.

## North Star

[One clear sentence describing what the product should become for the user.]

**EXAMPLE ONLY:**

## Trust Promise

[Describe what users should be able to trust about the product.]

Consider whether the product needs promises around:

- data ownership
- privacy
- local-first behavior
- account requirements
- sharing
- permissions
- auditability
- reliability
- accessibility
- security
- user control

**EXAMPLE ONLY:**

The product should feel safe because the user's content belongs to them. Data should be stored locally by default, without accounts, social feeds, follower graphs, algorithmic recommendations, or background sharing.

Sharing should be explicit and user-initiated. A person may choose to send an item or backup to someone else, but the product should not become a community platform or social network.

## Decision Filter

Before adding or expanding a feature, ask:

- Does this support the product's north star?
- Does this make the product more useful without making it feel unnecessarily heavier?
- Does this fit the intended user experience?
- Does this preserve the user's sense of safety, ownership, trust, and control?
- Can this be done simply enough that it preserves the intended feel of the product?

If the answer is not clearly yes, the idea belongs in the backlog, not the core experience.
